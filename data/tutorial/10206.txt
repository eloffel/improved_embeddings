abstract

   structural balance theory affirms that signed social networks (i.e.,
   graphs whose signed edges represent friendly/hostile interactions among
   individuals) tend to be organized so as to avoid conflictual
   situations, corresponding to cycles of negative parity. using an
   algorithm for ground-state calculation in large-scale ising spin
   glasses, in this paper we compute the global level of balance of very
   large online social networks and verify that currently available
   networks are indeed extremely balanced. this property is explainable in
   terms of the high degree of skewness of the sign distributions on the
   nodes of the graph. in particular, individuals linked by a large
   majority of negative edges create mostly    apparent disorder,    rather
   than true    frustration.
