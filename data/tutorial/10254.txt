abstract

   traditional citation analysis has been widely applied to detect
   patterns of scientific collaboration, map the landscapes of scholarly
   disciplines, assess the impact of research outputs, and observe
   knowledge transfer across domains. it is, however, limited, as it
   assumes all citations are of similar value and weights each equally.
   content   based citation analysis (cca) addresses a citation's value by
   interpreting each one based on its context at both the syntactic and
   semantic levels. this paper provides a comprehensive overview of caa
   research in terms of its theoretical foundations, methodical
   approaches, and example applications. in addition, we highlight how
   increased computational capabilities and publicly available full   text
   resources have opened this area of research to vast possibilities,
   which enable deeper citation analysis, more accurate citation
   prediction, and increased knowledge discovery.
