an index to quantify an individual   s scientific research output that takes
into account the effect of multiple coauthorship

     * authors
     * [11]authors and affiliations

     * j. e. hirsch[12] email author

   article
   first online: 13 march 2010
     * [13]2 shares
     * 1.9k downloads
     * [14]114 citations

abstract

   i propose the index \(\hbar\) (   hbar   ), defined as the number of papers
   of an individual that have citation count larger than or equal to the
   \(\hbar\) of all coauthors of each paper, as a useful index to
   characterize the scientific output of a researcher that takes into
   account the effect of multiple authorship. the bar is higher for
   \(\hbar.\)
