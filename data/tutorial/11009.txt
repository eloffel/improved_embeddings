abstract

   bibliometric measures of individual scientific achievement are of
   particular interest if they can be used to predict future achievement.
   here we report results of an empirical study of the predictive power of
   the h index compared with other indicators. our findings indicate that
   the h index is better than other indicators considered (total citation
   count, citations per paper, and total paper count) in predicting future
   scientific achievement. we discuss reasons for the superiority of the h
   index.
