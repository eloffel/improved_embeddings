abstract

   this paper studies how varied damping factors in the id95 algorithm
   influence the ranking of authors and proposes weighted id95
   algorithms. we selected the 108 most highly cited authors in the
   information retrieval (ir) area from the 1970s to 2008 to form the
   author co   citation network. we calculated the ranks of these 108
   authors based on id95 with the damping factor ranging from 0.05 to
   0.95. in order to test the relationship between different measures, we
   compared id95 and weighted id95 results with the citation
   ranking, h   index, and centrality measures. we found that in our author
   co   citation network, citation rank is highly correlated with id95
   with different damping factors and also with different weighted
   id95 algorithms; citation rank and id95 are not significantly
   correlated with centrality measures; and h   index rank does not
   significantly correlate with centrality measures but does significantly
   correlate with other measures. the key factors that have impact on the
   id95 of authors in the author co   citation network are being
   co   cited with important authors.
