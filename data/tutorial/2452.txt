datasets

   these datasets can be used for benchmarking deep learning algorithms:

   music datasets
     __________________________________________________________________

     * piano-midi.de: classical piano pieces
       ([63]http://www.piano-midi.de/)
     * nottingham : over 1000 folk tunes
       ([64]http://abc.sourceforge.net/nmd/)
     * musedata: electronic library of classical music scores
       ([65]http://musedata.stanford.edu/)
     * jsb chorales: set of four-part harmonized chorales
       ([66]http://www.jsbchorales.net/index.shtml)
     * fma: a dataset for music analysis
       ([67]https://github.com/mdeff/fma)

   natural images
     __________________________________________________________________

     * mnist: handwritten digits ([68]http://yann.lecun.com/exdb/mnist/)
     * nist: similar to mnist, but larger
     * perturbed nist: a dataset developed in yoshua   s class (nist with
       tons of deformations)
     * cifar10 / cifar100: 32  32 natural image dataset with 10/100
       categories ( [69]http://www.cs.utoronto.ca/~kriz/cifar.html)
     * caltech 101: pictures of objects belonging to 101 categories
       ([70]http://www.vision.caltech.edu/image_datasets/caltech101/)
     * caltech 256: pictures of objects belonging to 256 categories
       ([71]http://www.vision.caltech.edu/image_datasets/caltech256/)
     * caltech silhouettes: 28  28 binary images contains silhouettes of
       the caltech 101 dataset
     * stl-10 dataset is an image recognition dataset for developing
       unsupervised id171, deep learning, self-taught learning
       algorithms. it is inspired by the [72]cifar-10 dataset but with
       some modifications. [73]http://www.stanford.edu/~acoates//stl10/
     * the street view house numbers (svhn) dataset
           [74]http://ufldl.stanford.edu/housenumbers/
     * norb: binocular images of toy figurines under various illumination
       and pose ([75]http://www.cs.nyu.edu/~ylclab/data/norb-v1.0/)
     * id163: image database organized according to the
       id138hierarchy ([76]http://www.image-net.org/)
     * pascal voc: various object recognition challenges
       ([77]http://pascallin.ecs.soton.ac.uk/challenges/voc/)
     * labelme: a large dataset of annotated
       images, [78]http://labelme.csail.mit.edu/release3.0/browsertools/ph
       p/dataset.php
     * coil 20: different objects imaged at every angle in a 360
       rotation([79]http://www.cs.columbia.edu/cave/software/softlib/coil-
       20.php)
     * coil100: different objects imaged at every angle in a 360 rotation
       ([80]http://www1.cs.columbia.edu/cave/software/softlib/coil-100.php
       )

   artificial datasets
     __________________________________________________________________

     * [81]arcade universe     an artificial dataset generator with images
       containing arcade games sprites such as tetris pentomino/tetromino
       objects. this generator is based on the o. breleux   s [82]bugland
       dataset generator.
     * a collection of datasets inspired by the ideas from
       [83]babyaischool:
          + [84]babyaishapesdatasets : distinguishing between 3 simple
            shapes
          + [85]babyaiimageandquestiondatasets : a question-image-answer
            dataset
     * datasets generated for the purpose of an empirical evaluation of
       deep architectures ([86]deepvsshallowcomparisonicml2007):
          + [87]mnistvariations : introducing controlled variations in
            mnist
          + [88]rectanglesdata : discriminating between wide and tall
            rectangles
          + [89]convexnonconvex : discriminating between convex and
            nonconvex shapes
          + [90]backgroundcorrelation : controlling the degree of
            correlation in noisy mnist backgrounds

   faces
     __________________________________________________________________

     * labelled faces in the wild: 13,000 images of faces collected from
       the web, labelled with the name of the person pictured
       ([91]http://vis-www.cs.umass.edu/lfw/)
     * toronto face dataset
     * olivetti: a few images of several different people
       ([92]http://www.cs.nyu.edu/~roweis/data.html)
     * multi-pie: the cmu multi-pie face database
       ([93]http://www.multipie.org/)
     * face-in-action ([94]http://www.flintbox.com/public/project/5486/)
     * jacfee: japanese and caucasian facial expressions of emotion
       ([95]http://www.humintell.com/jacfee/)
     * feret: the facial recognition technology database
       ([96]http://www.itl.nist.gov/iad/humanid/feret/feret_master.html)
     * mmifacedb: mmi facial expression database
       ([97]http://www.mmifacedb.com/)
     * indianfacedatabase:
       [98]http://vis-www.cs.umass.edu/~vidit/indianfacedatabase/)
     * (e.g. the yale face database
       ([99]http://vision.ucsd.edu/content/yale-face-database) and the
       yale face database b
       ([100]http://vision.ucsd.edu/~leekc/extyaledatabase/extyaleb.html))
       .

   text
     __________________________________________________________________

     * 20 newsgroups: classification task, mapping word occurences to
       newsgroup id ([101]http://qwone.com/~jason/20newsgroups/)
     * reuters (rcv*) corpuses: text/topic prediction
       ([102]http://about.reuters.com/researchandstandards/corpus/)
     * id32 : used for next word prediction or next character
       prediction ([103]http://www.cis.upenn.edu/~treebank/)
     * broadcast news: large text dataset, classically used for next word
       prediction
       ([104]http://www.ldc.upenn.edu/catalog/catalogentry.jsp?catalogid=l
       dc97s44)
     * wikipedia dataset
     * multidomain id31
       dataset: [105]http://www.cs.jhu.edu/~mdredze/datasets/sentiment/

   speech
     __________________________________________________________________

     * timit speech corpus: phoneme classification
       ([106]http://www.ldc.upenn.edu/catalog/catalogentry.jsp?catalogid=l
       dc93s1)
     * aurora : timit with noise and additional information

   id126s
     __________________________________________________________________

     * movielens: two datasets available
       from [107]http://www.grouplens.org. the first dataset has 100,000
       ratings for 1682 movies by 943 users, subdivided into five disjoint
       subsets. the second dataset has about 1 million ratings for 3900
       movies by 6040 users.
     * jester: this [108]dataset contains 4.1 million continuous ratings
       (-10.00 to +10.00) of 100 jokes from 73,421 users.
     * netflix prize: netflix released an anonymised version of their
       movie rating [109]dataset; it consists of 100 million ratings, done
       by 480,000 users who have rated between 1 and all of the 17,770
       movies.
     * book-crossing dataset: this [110]dataset is from the book-crossing
       community, and contains 278,858 users providing 1,149,780 ratings
       about 271,379 books.

   misc
     __________________________________________________________________

     *    musk    dataset
     * cmu motion capture database: ([111]http://mocap.cs.cmu.edu/)
     * brodatz dataset: texture modeling
       ([112]http://www.ux.uis.no/~tranden/brodatz.html)
     * million song
       dataset: [113]http://labrosa.ee.columbia.edu/millionsong/
     * merck molecular activity challenge
           [114]http://www.kaggle.com/c/merckactivity/data
