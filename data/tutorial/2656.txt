   machine learning course - recorded at a live broadcast from caltech

the lectures

     * taught by feynman prize winner professor yaser abu-mostafa.
     * the fundamental concepts and techniques are explained in detail.
       the focus of the lectures is real understanding, not just
       "knowing."
     * lectures use incremental viewgraphs (2853 in total) to simulate the
       pace of blackboard teaching.
     * the 18 lectures (below) are available on different platforms:

                     here is the playlist on [3]youtube

   lectures are available on [4]itunes u course app

   place the mouse on a lecture title for a short description

     * lecture 1 (the learning problem)
       [5]lecture (some audio drops, sorry!) - [6]q&a - [7]slides
       the learning problem - introduction; supervised, unsupervised, and
       id23. components of the learning problem.
     * lecture 2 (is learning feasible?)
       [8]review - [9]lecture - [10]q&a - [11]slides
       is learning feasible? - can we generalize from a limited sample to
       the entire space? relationship between in-sample and out-of-sample.
     * lecture 3 (the linear model i)
       [12]review - [13]lecture - [14]q&a - [15]slides
       the linear model i - linear classification and id75.
       extending linear models through nonlinear transforms.
     * lecture 4 (error and noise)
       [16]review - [17]lecture - [18]q&a - [19]slides
       error and noise - the principled choice of error measures. what
       happens when the target we want to learn is noisy.
     * lecture 5 (training versus testing)
       [20]review - [21]lecture - [22]q&a - [23]slides
       training versus testing - the difference between training and
       testing in mathematical terms. what makes a learning model able to
       generalize?
     * lecture 6 (theory of generalization)
       [24]review - [25]lecture - [26]q&a - [27]slides
       theory of generalization - how an infinite model can learn from a
       finite sample. the most important theoretical result in machine
       learning.
     * lecture 7 (the vc dimension)
       [28]review - [29]lecture - [30]q&a - [31]slides
       the vc dimension - a measure of what it takes a model to learn.
       relationship to the number of parameters and degrees of freedom.
     * lecture 8 (id160)
       [32]review - [33]lecture - [34]q&a - [35]slides
       id160 - breaking down the learning performance
       into competing quantities. the learning curves.
     * lecture 9 (the linear model ii)
       [36]review - [37]lecture - [38]q&a - [39]slides
       the linear model ii - more about linear models. logistic
       regression, maximum likelihood, and id119.
     * lecture 10 (neural networks)
       [40]review - [41]lecture - [42]q&a - [43]slides
       neural networks - a biologically inspired model. the efficient
       id26 learning algorithm. hidden layers.
     * lecture 11 (overfitting)
       [44]review - [45]lecture - [46]q&a - [47]slides
       overfitting - fitting the data too well; fitting the noise.
       deterministic noise versus stochastic noise.
     * lecture 12 (id173)
       [48]review - [49]lecture - [50]q&a - [51]slides
       id173 - putting the brakes on fitting the noise. hard and
       soft constraints. augmented error and weight decay.
     * lecture 13 (validation)
       [52]review - [53]lecture - [54]q&a - [55]slides
       validation - taking a peek out of sample. model selection and data
       contamination. cross validation.
     * lecture 14 (support vector machines)
       [56]review - [57]lecture - [58]q&a - [59]slides
       support vector machines - one of the most successful learning
       algorithms; getting a complex model at the price of a simple one.
     * lecture 15 (kernel methods)
       [60]review - [61]lecture - [62]q&a - [63]slides
       kernel methods - extending id166 to infinite-dimensional spaces using
       the kernel trick, and to non-separable data using soft margins.
     * lecture 16 (radial basis functions)
       [64]review - [65]lecture - [66]q&a - [67]slides
       radial basis functions - an important learning model that connects
       several machine learning models and techniques.
     * lecture 17 (three learning principles)
       [68]review - [69]lecture - [70]q&a - [71]slides
       three learning principles - major pitfalls for machine learning
       practitioners; occam's razor, sampling bias, and data snooping.
     * lecture 18 (epilogue)
       [72]review - [73]lecture - [74]acknowledgment - [75]slides
       epilogue - the map of machine learning. brief views of bayesian
       learning and aggregation methods.
