readme.md

   note: i don't provide personal support for custom changes in the code.
   only for the release. for people just starting, i recommend
   [73]treehouse for online-learning.

naive bayes id166 (nb-id166)

   this code reproduces performance of the nb-id166 on the imdb reviews from
   the paper:

   sida wang and christopher d. manning: baselines and bigrams: simple,
   good sentiment and topic classification; acl 2012.
   [74]http://nlp.stanford.edu/pubs/sidaw12_simple_sentiment.pdf

   they obtain 91.22% while this code obtains 91.55% with bigrams and
   91.82% with trigrams. little improvements (+0.33% with bigrams and
   +0.6% with unigrams) versus the paper.

   to reproduce the results:
git clone git@github.com:mesnilgr/nbid166.git
cd nbid166; chmod +x oh_my_go.sh
./oh_my_go.sh

   end to end (downloading the data, tokenizing, training the models),
   this will take 68 mins. note that most of the time is spent dowloading
   and tokenizing. once the data has been downloaded and tokenized,
   training an nb-id166 only takes ~2 mins for uni+bigrams and <5 mins for
   uni+bi+trigrams.
