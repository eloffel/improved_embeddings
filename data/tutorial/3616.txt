readme.md

uni-dep-tb

   a set of treebanks for multiple languages annotated in basic
   stanford-style dependencies.

   nb: the guidelines are currently under revision and the project is
   migrating to [70]http://universaldependencies.github.io/docs/. for
   further updates, check the new site or contact the project
   coordinators.

   v2.0
     * includes brazilian-portuguese, english, finnish, french, german,
       italian, indonesian, japanese, korean, spanish and swedish
     * beta content-head version
     * bug fixes
     * description of universal relations

   v1.0
     * includes english, french, german, spanish, swedish and korean.

   releases
     * version 2.0 - bug fixes, new data, 5 new languages, content-head
       beta version
     * version 1.0 - initial release

   relevant documents
     * universal dependency guidelines
          + [71]https://uni-dep-tb.googlecode.com/svn/trunk/universal-guid
            elines.pdf
     * universal dependency annotation for multilingual parsing. mcdonald
       et al. acl 2013
          + [72]http://ryanmcd.com/papers/treebanksacl2013.pdf
     * english stanford guidelines
          + [73]http://www-nlp.stanford.edu/software/dependencies_manual.p
            df
     * generating typed dependency parses from phrase structure parses. de
       marneffe et al. lrec 2006.
          + [74]https://code.google.com/p/uni-dep-tb/

   contributors and acknowledgements
     * project coordinators: ryan mcdonald, joakim nivre, slav petrov
     * data contributors include yvonne quirmbach-brundage and others at
       appen-butler-hill; adam lamontagne, milan soucek, timo jarvinen,
       alessandra radici and others at lionbridge
     * joakim nivre provided the harmonized version of the swedish
       treebank talbanken portion
          + [75]http://stp.lingfil.uu.se/~nivre/swedish_treebank/
     * filip ginter and the group at turku provided the finnish data and
       assisted in the harmonization process
          + [76]http://bionlp.utu.fi/fintreebank.html
     * maria simi and other researchers at pisa provided the harmonized
       italian data
          + [77]http://medialab.di.unipi.it/wiki/isdt
     * thanks to fernando pereira, alfred spector, dave orr, jennifer bahk
       and others at google for support.
     * thanks to hans uszkoreit for giving us permission to use sentences
       from the tiger treebank.
