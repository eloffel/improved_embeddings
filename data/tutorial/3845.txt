summary

   numerical python by robert johansson shows you how to leverage the
   numerical and mathematical capabilities in python, its standard
   library, and the extensive ecosystem of computationally oriented python
   libraries, including popular packages such as numpy, scipy, sympy,
   matplotlib, pandas, and more, and how to apply these software tools in
   computational problem solving.

   python has gained widespread popularity as a computing language: it is
   nowadays employed for computing by practitioners in such diverse fields
   as for example scientific research, engineering, finance, and data
   analytics. one reason for the popularity of python is its high-level
   and easy-to-work-with syntax, which enables the rapid development and
   exploratory computing that is required in modern computational work.

   after reading and using this book, you will have seen examples and case
   studies from many areas of computing, and gained familiarity with basic
   computing techniques such as array-based and symbolic computing,
   all-around practical skills such as visualisation and numerical file
   i/o, general computational methods such as equation solving,
   optimization, interpolation and integration, and domain-specific
   computational problems, such as differential equation solving, data
   analysis, statistical modeling and machine learning.
