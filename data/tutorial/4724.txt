   published on aug 2, 2017

   abstract: i describe research in our lab on computationally extracting
   social meaning from language, meaning that takes into account social
   relationships between people. i describe our study of interactions
   between police and community members in traffic stops recorded in body-
   worn camera footage. we automatically measure the quality of the
   interaction from language, study the role of race in the interaction,
   and draw suggestions for going forward in this fraught area. in another
   we computationally model the language of scientific papers together
   with the network formed by scientists and their research areas to
   better understand scientific innovation, how it progresses, and the
   role of interdisciplinarity. i discuss implications for the history of
   science and specifically of artificial intelligence. both studies
   highlight the importance of social context and social models for
   interpreting the latent meanings behind the words we use.
