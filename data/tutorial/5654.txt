   beja    is the arabic name for the language which calls itself    ti bedawye   , the unwritten language of a group of 
mainly nomadic tribes that have probably occupied the north-east corner of the sudan (between the nile and 
the red sea) for thousands of years. it is classified as an    afro-asiatic    language, which means that it is distant-
ly related to arabic, hebrew, and ancient egyptian. in the following examples,     stands for a glottal stop (the
