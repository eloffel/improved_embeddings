yago: a high-quality knowledge base

   yago is a joint project of the [79]max planck institute for informatics
   and the [80]telecom paristech university.

yago: overview


   yago is a huge semantic knowledge base, derived
   from [81]wikipedia [82]id138 and [83]geonames. currently, yago has
   knowledge of more than 10 million entities (like persons,
   organizations, cities, etc.) and contains more than 120 million facts
   about these entities.

   yago is special in several ways:
    1. the accuracy of yago has been manually evaluated, proving a
       confirmed accuracy of 95%. every relation is annotated with its
       confidence value.
    2. yago combines the clean taxonomy of [84]id138 with the richness
       of the [85]wikipedia category system, assigning the entities to
       more than 350,000 classes.
    3. yago is an ontology that is anchored in time and space. yago
       attaches a temporal dimension and a spacial dimension to many of
       its facts and entities.
    4. in addition to a taxonomy, yago has thematic domains such as
       "music" or "science" from [86]id138 domains.
    5. yago extracts and combines entities and facts from 10 wikipedias in
       different languages.

   yago is developed jointly with the [87]dig group at [88]t  l  com
   paristech university.

       the yago code is now available for anyone to work with. [91]get it
       on github!

yago is part of the [136]yago-naga project at the [137]max planck institute
for informatics in saarbr  cken/germany. it is maintained jointly by
the [138]databases and information systems group and the [139]dbweb team of
t  l  com paristech.
