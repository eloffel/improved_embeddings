semantic medline database

   the semantic medline database (semmeddb) [1] is a repository of
   semantic predications (subject-predicate-object triples) extracted by
   semrep, a semantic interpreter of biomedical text [2]. semmeddb
   currently contains information about approximately 94.0 million
   predications from all of pubmed citations (about 27.9 million
   citations, as of december 31 2017) and forms the backbone of the
   [2]semantic medline application [3, 4].

   for details about the semmeddb schema, click [3]here.

   to download the semmeddb, a uts account is required. users that already
   have one need to click [4]here and provide their uts account
   information. to get a uts account, click [5]here.

   for more information about semmeddb, contact [6]semmed@nlm.nih.gov.
