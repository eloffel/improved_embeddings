title:tutorial on id5

   authors:[27]carl doersch
   (submitted on 19 jun 2016 ([28]v1), last revised 13 aug 2016 (this
   version, v2))

     abstract: in just three years, id5 (vaes) have
     emerged as one of the most popular approaches to unsupervised
     learning of complicated distributions. vaes are appealing because
     they are built on top of standard function approximators (neural
     networks), and can be trained with stochastic id119. vaes
     have already shown promise in generating many kinds of complicated
     data, including handwritten digits, faces, house numbers, cifar
     images, physical models of scenes, segmentation, and predicting the
     future from static images. this tutorial introduces the intuitions
     behind vaes, explains the mathematics behind them, and describes
     some empirical behavior. no prior knowledge of id58ian
     methods is assumed.

   subjects: machine learning (stat.ml); machine learning (cs.lg)
